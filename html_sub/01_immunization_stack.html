FACT ABOUT IMMUNIZATION

1. What is Immunization?
Immunization, also known as vaccination, is the process of receiving a vaccine to stimulate the immune system and protect against certain infectious diseases. Vaccines work by exposing the body to a weakened or killed form of the virus or bacteria, which helps the immune system build up a defense against the disease.

2. Why is Immunization important?
Immunization is important because it can protect individuals and communities from diseases that can cause serious illness, hospitalization, and even death. By getting vaccinated, you can help stop the spread of infectious diseases and keep yourself and those around you healthy. For children, Immunizations are important because they protect your child from serious illnesses that can cause lasting damage or even death. They also help protect other children and adults in your community who may not be able to get vaccinated themselves, such as those with weakened immune systems.

3. Who should get vaccinated?
Vaccines are recommended for people of all ages, from infants to seniors. Depending on your age, health status, and other factors, you may need different vaccines at different times in your life. Your healthcare provider can help you determine which vaccines are right for you.

4. When should my child get Immunized?
The National Primary Health Care Development Agency (NPHCDA) and Centres for Disease Control and Prevention recommends specific immunization schedule for children based on their age and women. At Irowa Medical Centres, we provide you with accessible personalized immunization services in line with these schedule and other specific vaccination according to global best practices. It is important vaccine are given timely and in accurate doses.

5. What should I do if my child misses an immunization?
If your child misses an immunization, don't panic! Simply talk to your doctor to schedule a catch-up appointment as soon as possible. It's never too late to get your child up-to-date on their immunizations.
